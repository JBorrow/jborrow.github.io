About

I am a PhD student at the Institute for Computational Cosmology at the University of Durham, UK. My current work includes:

  • Leading the analysis pipeline for the next generation of EAGLE simulations with SWIFT (see below) leveraging big data and high performance computing.
  • Creating novel Lagrangian CFD (SPH) schemes for use in the modelling of astrophysical fluids.
  • Error analysis of sub-grid models within cosmological hydrodynamical simulations.

I currently work on the SWIFT code, which is a modern, extensible cosmological simulation code. With SWIFT, we hope to improve (by at least an order of magnitude in box-size, keeping resolution the same) on the highly successful EAGLE Project.

I am also heavily involved with our outreach programmes here at Durham, and created the Galaxy Makers which was a new leap forward in re-engagement practices for exhibition-style engagement events.
